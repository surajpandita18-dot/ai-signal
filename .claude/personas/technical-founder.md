# Persona: The Technical Founder

**Name:** Arjun / Sasha / Marcus (archetype, not individual)

**Role:** CTO / technical co-founder at a seed–Series A AI startup

**Team size:** 2–8 engineers

**Stage:** Post-PMF search, pre-revenue scale. Has shipped a product. Has paying customers. Making daily technical decisions that compound.

---

## Day in the life

- 6:30am: checks phone in bed — Twitter/X, Hacker News, one or two newsletters
- 9am: standup with team. Makes decisions about what to build this sprint
- Throughout day: context-switches between coding, customer calls, hiring, fundraising
- Reads async — no time for long-form during the day
- Catches up on news after 9pm or early morning
- **Total time for AI news: 5–10 minutes per day, max**

---

## Current information diet

- Twitter/X: curated follows of AI researchers and founders
- Hacker News: "Ask HN" threads, major model releases
- Newsletter: usually unread by Thursday, archived by Sunday
- Slack communities: signal-to-noise too low
- **Problem:** everything covers the same 3 stories. Nothing tells you what it means for *your* stack.

---

## Jobs-to-be-done

**Primary:** "Tell me if anything shipped in the last 24 hours that obsoletes a decision I made last week."

**Secondary:** "Confirm I'm not missing something my competitors already know about."

**Tertiary:** "Give me something concrete to bring to standup that sounds smart."

---

## Fears

- Building on a model that gets deprecated or price-shocked
- Competitor shipping a feature that makes their differentiator irrelevant
- Missing an open-source release that their paid SaaS is now blocking
- Being the last to know about something their investors ask about

---

## Triggers to upgrade

- Reads a TAKEAWAY that directly changes a decision they were about to make
- Comes back 3+ days in a row — product is in their morning routine
- Sees a signal they wish they'd seen 2 weeks earlier

---

## Objections to upgrade

- "I can get this from Twitter for free"
- "I don't know if I'll remember to check it daily"
- "The summaries feel generic" (if they do)
- "It's another subscription I have to justify"

---

## Verbatim language they use

- "What actually shipped?"
- "Is this real or just hype?"
- "What does this mean for what I'm building?"
- "Should I be worried about this?"
- "Is this a threat or an opportunity?"
