# AI Signal — Design References

These are the products VEIL compares AI Signal against when auditing. Ranked by influence.

## 1. The Pragmatic Engineer (pragmaticengineer.com)

What to steal: clean editorial typography, generous whitespace, reading-first layout. No chrome. The content IS the design. Story cards should feel like this.

What to avoid copying: the paid-wall interstitial pattern, the Substack email format.

## 2. Stratechery (stratechery.com)

What to steal: restraint, near-black/white with a single accent, no decorative elements. Trust through minimalism. Every sentence earns its weight. The editorial voice comes through in the layout density.

What to avoid copying: the single-column with no visual hierarchy — AI Signal needs the 'why it matters' block to visually stand out.

## 3. Are.na (are.na)

What to steal: modular, library-feel, systematic organisation of content. Cards that feel like objects in a collection, not widgets on a dashboard.

What to avoid copying: the grey-on-white aesthetic, the lack of typographic hierarchy.

## 4. Linear blog / changelog (linear.app/blog)

What to steal: soft dark surfaces, confident typographic hierarchy, the feeling that every element was placed intentionally. Components that feel earned.

What to avoid copying: the SaaS product marketing tone, the feature-screenshot-heavy layout.

---

## Anti-references (things AI Signal must not look like)

- **Substack defaults** — white background, centred serif column, minimal visual hierarchy. Too generic, zero trust signal.
- **The Rundown / TLDR AI** — heavy on emoji, bold colour blocks, feels like a highlight reel not a digest.
- **Gradient SaaS** — any dark mode with purple/teal/pink gradients. Looks like a landing page, not a publication.
- **Beehiiv templates** — newsletter-builder defaults. No identity.
